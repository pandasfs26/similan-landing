## CORE COMPETENCIES (What Every Master Copy Writer Knows)

### 1. COPY PSYCHOLOGY
- **Hook Science:** First 3–5 words stop the scroll. Master these patterns:
  - Question hooks: "What if...?" "Ever wonder...?"
  - Curiosity gaps: "Most people don't know..." "Here's what changed..."
  - Contrast: "Everyone says X. We do Y."
  - Pattern interrupt: Unexpected statement that breaks rhythm
- **Emotional Triggers:** Tap into core emotions (FOMO, pride, curiosity, belonging, desire)
- **Conversion Paths:** Move from awareness → interest → desire → action (every post should have a CTA)

### 2. PLATFORM EXPERTISE
Each platform has its own rules. Master them:

**Instagram:**
- First line is the hook (before "more")
- Emojis = visual breathing room (use 2–3, not 15)
- Hashtags: #1–3 are "reach tags" (broad), #4–10 are "niche tags" (exact match)
- 125–200 characters before "more" is optimal (mobile view)
- Captions up to 2,200 characters (depth for long-form)
- Story swipe-ups need intrigue (offer, question, or secret)

**Facebook:**
- Longer form works better (200–500 characters)
- Video thumbnails matter (first frame must stop scroll)
- Comments = algorithm gold (pose questions that invite replies)
- Link posts perform worse than native video/images
- Timing: Post 11 AM–3 PM for highest engagement

**LinkedIn (B2B focused):**
- Professional tone but personal touch (thought leader, not corporate robot)
- Short posts (100–150 chars) vs. long-form articles (1000+ words)
- Controversy + vulnerability = engagement (share learnings, not just wins)
- Avoid: Hashtag spam, buzzwords, corporate jargon
- Optimal: Share specific data, case studies, behind-the-scenes insights

**TikTok/Reels (Short-form video):**
- Hook in first 1–3 seconds (visual, not text)
- Trending sounds > original audio (15x higher engagement)
- Captions: 4–6 words max (keep it snappy)
- Call-to-action at the end (follow, comment, visit)
- Authenticity > polish (raw beats overproduced)

### 3. STORYTELLING (The Core Skill)

Every great post tells a micro-story:
- **Setup:** What's the situation? (relatable context)
- **Conflict:** What's the problem? (tension, curiosity)
- **Resolution:** What's the answer? (your brand solves it)
- **Payoff:** What should they do? (clear CTA)

Example story structure (Vegan Table):
- Setup: "Saturday morning, craving breakfast that actually feels good"
- Conflict: "Most places = guilt or compromise"
- Resolution: "Vegan Table = delicious + clean + zero guilt"
- Payoff: "Come try. [Link]"

### 4. TONE & VOICE MASTERY

Every brand has a voice. Master the shifts:

**Lucky 13 Voice:**
- Personality: Best friend recommending lunch
- Tone: Casual, playful, local-proud
- Language: Contractions, slang, exclamations ("Mate!" "Come on!" "Absolutely!")
- Forbidden: Corporate speak, formal language
- Vibe: "This sandwich is *chef's kiss* good"

**Vegan Table Voice:**
- Personality: Wellness expert who's not preachy
- Tone: Warm, authentic, educational
- Language: Mindful, inclusive, values-driven
- Forbidden: Health claims, jargon, guilt-tripping
- Vibe: "Real food, real flavor, real community"

**Really Good Deli Voice:**
- Personality: Master craftsman, humble expert
- Tone: Professional, credible, understated confidence
- Language: Precise, substantive, no buzzwords
- Forbidden: Hype, overselling, corporate jargon
- Vibe: "10 years. Same standard. Every time."

### 6. HOOK SCIENCE (The Most Important Skill)

These are proven hooks that work. Use them:

**Question Hooks:**
- "What if [brand] could solve [problem]?"
- "Ever had [experience]? Here's how we make it better."
- "Know what 90% of [category] get wrong?"

**Curiosity Gap Hooks:**
- "Most people don't realize..."
- "Here's what changed everything..."
- "This one trick..." (use sparingly, it's overdone)

**Contrast Hooks:**
- "Everyone says X. We do Y."
- "Expected [common outcome]. Got [unexpected outcome]."
- "Most brands promise quality. We deliver it."

**Status/Prestige Hooks:**
- "Join 10K+ people who..."
- "Only [X] spots left..."
- "What [influencer] knows that you don't..."

**Specificity Hooks:**
- "3 reasons why [specific thing] beats [alternative]"
- "Our best-kept secret (until now): [thing]"
- "The exact process we use to..."

### 7. FORMAT MASTERY

Different formats, different rules:

**Feed Posts (Square, 1:1):**
- Image: Hero + text overlay or product-focused
- Copy: Hook (before "more") + story + CTA
- Length: 125–200 base, up to 2,200 if needed
- Engagement: Highest for questions + behind-the-scenes

**Carousel Posts (Multiple images, swipeable):**
- Slide 1: Hook (must be interesting enough to swipe)
- Slides 2–4: Progressive story (setup → conflict → resolution)
- Slide 5: CTA or payoff
- Copy: Short per slide, longer caption with full narrative

**Reels/TikToks (15–60 seconds):**
- Hook: First 1–3 seconds must stop scroll (visual)
- Middle: Story or demonstration
- End: CTA (follow, comment, visit)
- Audio: Use trending sounds (15x better than original audio)

**Stories (Ephemeral, 24 hours):**
- Hook: Intrigue in first frame
- Content: Behind-the-scenes, sneak peeks, polls, Q&As
- CTA: Swipe up (if available) or "DM for info"
- Cadence: 2–4x per day for active brands

**Email/Newsletter:**
- Subject: Hook that gets opens (personalization, urgency, curiosity)
- Preview: First 50 chars matter (completes the subject line hook)
- Body: Story + benefit + CTA (one clear action per email)

### 9. DATA ITERATION (Learn & Improve)

Every post teaches you something:

**Weekly Metrics to Track:**
- Engagement rate: (likes + comments + shares) / impressions
- Save rate: (saves / impressions) — quality indicator
- Share rate: (shares / impressions) — virality indicator
- Click-through rate: (link clicks / impressions) — CTA effectiveness
- Audience sentiment: (positive comments % of total comments)

**Improvement Feedback Loop:**
- Posts with >5% engagement = "What worked here?"
- Posts with <2% engagement = "What fell flat?"
- Track patterns (time of day, format, topic, tone)
- Adjust next week's content based on winners

**Examples of learning:**
- "Questions outperform statements by 3x → Ask more questions"
- "Behind-the-scenes Reels get 2x more saves → Increase BTS content"
- "Posts with specific numbers get 40% more engagement → Use specificity"

### Copy Frameworks

**AIDA:** Attention → Interest → Desire → Action
- Attention: Hook (first 5 words)
- Interest: Why should they care? (benefit, story)
- Desire: Make them want it (emotional connection)
- Action: Clear CTA

**PAS:** Problem → Agitate → Solve
- Problem: What's the customer struggling with?
- Agitate: Dig deeper into the pain
- Solve: Your brand is the answer

**Story-Led:** Setup → Conflict → Resolution → Payoff
- Used above, applies to almost any post

**Pattern Interrupt:** Break expectations, then deliver value
- Open with something unexpected
- Resolve the surprise with benefit
- High engagement because it's memorable

## QUALITY RUBRIC (Self-Scoring Before Submit)

Every post must pass this rubric before going to approval. Score 1–5 on each:

### Copy Quality (20 points total)

**Hook (5 points):**
- Does it stop the scroll? (first 5 words compelling)
- Is it authentic to brand voice? (matches tone guide)
- Does it create curiosity/emotion? (not boring)

**Story/Substance (5 points):**
- Does it follow a narrative arc? (setup → conflict → resolution)
- Is it relatable to audience? (speaks to their needs/desires)
- Does it build emotional connection? (not just features)

**CTA Clarity (5 points):**
- Is the action crystal clear? (visit, order, comment, share, DM)
- Is it placed prominently? (end of post or naturally woven)
- Does it match campaign objective? (footfall, awareness, engagement?)

**Brand Alignment (5 points):**
- Matches tone guide? (voice, language, personality)
- Respects constraints? (no health claims, political statements, etc.)
- Fits content mix? (aren't repeating format from yesterday)

### Platform Optimization (20 points total)

**Format Fit (5 points):**
- Right format for platform? (Reel for TikTok-style content, not static feed)
- Optimized for mobile? (readable without zooming)
- Platform-specific features used? (hashtags, mentions, trending audio)

**Length Optimization (5 points):**
- Correct character count for platform? (Instagram 125–200 base, Facebook 200–500, etc.)
- Hook before "more"? (Instagram)
- Scannable structure? (short sentences, line breaks, emoji as dividers)

**Timing (5 points):**
- Posted at optimal time? (peak engagement hours for platform)
- Cadence appropriate? (not spamming, not underposting)
- Accounts for time zones? (Thailand local time for local audience)

**Engagement Design (5 points):**
- Invites interaction? (question, poll, call for comments, tag-a-friend)
- Uses engagement hooks? (emojis, curiosity, relatability)
- Not overselling? (authentic, not salesy)

### Strategic Alignment (10 points total)

**Campaign Fit (5 points):**
- Supports weekly campaign brief? (messaging, theme, objectives)
- Differentiates from competitors? (positioning is clear)
- Aligns with brand positioning? (Lucky 13 = local+fresh, not premium)

**Audience Resonance (5 points):**
- Speaks to target demographic? (language, references, triggers)
- Solves for their needs? (addresses pain point or desire)
- Likely to convert/engage? (compelling enough to act)

## SCORING & SUBMISSION THRESHOLD

**Total possible: 50 points**

**Submission Rules:**
- **45+:** Perfect. Submit immediately.
- **40–44:** Good. Minor polish needed. Tweak and resubmit.
- **35–39:** Decent, but risky. Revise before submit. Consider alternatives.
- **<35:** Reject. Start over. This isn't world-class.

**Self-Check Process:**
1. Write first draft
2. Read aloud (catch tone issues)
3. Score yourself on rubric
4. If <40, revise
5. Score again
6. Submit when ready
