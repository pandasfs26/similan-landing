# Competitor Audit: Similan Digital's Strategic Positioning

**Research Date:** March 14, 2026  
**Brands Analyzed:** 5 direct competitors across 3 client brands  
**Purpose:** Identify gaps, opportunities, and differentiation angles

---

## LUCKY 13 SANDWICH vs COMPETITORS

### Competitor 1: PRIME BURGER (https://primeburger.co.th/)

**Social Presence:**
- Instagram: ~8K followers (est.)
- Facebook: ~12K followers
- TikTok: Active (~2K followers)
- Posting cadence: 4–5 posts/week (consistent)

**Content Strategy:**
- Primary: Product photography (burger close-ups, appetizing styling)
- Secondary: Location updates, new menu items, promotions
- Tertiary: Behind-the-scenes, team culture (minimal)
- Tone: Premium, aspirational, trendy ("Quality Burgers for Quality People")

**Engagement Analysis:**
- Average post likes: 150–300
- Engagement rate: ~3–4% (healthy)
- Comment sentiment: Positive (compliments, requests for new locations)
- User-generated content: Minimal (customers don't tag much)

**Strengths:**
- Clean, professional visual brand
- Consistent high-quality photography
- Strong location expansion messaging (aspirational)
- Trendy positioning (premium burgers, not casual)

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited storytelling (mostly product photos)
- No real community engagement (one-way broadcasting)
- Doesn't highlight local/sustainability angle
- Minimal behind-the-scenes or team visibility
- No thought leadership or humor

**Opportunity for Lucky 13:**
- **Angle 1:** Local pride vs. premium trend
  - Prime Burger = "Quality burgers for quality people" (aspirational, premium, distant)
  - Lucky 13 = "Fresh sandwiches made by your neighbors" (authentic, local, community)
  
- **Angle 2:** Storytelling vs. product photos
  - Prime Burger: Show burger, show price, sell burger
  - Lucky 13: Show the farmer who grew the lettuce, show the customer who loves it, build connection
  
- **Angle 3:** Fun & approachable vs. trendy & premium
  - Prime Burger: Professional, sleek, intimidating
  - Lucky 13: Casual, friendly, "this is for everyone"

### Competitor 2: BARTELS THAILAND (https://bartelsthailand.com/)

**Social Presence:**
- Instagram: ~4.5K followers (est.)
- Facebook: ~6K followers
- Website: Strong (e-commerce, food blog, recipes)
- Posting cadence: 2–3 posts/week (less frequent)

**Content Strategy:**
- Primary: Product features (artisanal sourdough bread, premium ingredients)
- Secondary: Baking process, ingredient sourcing, how-to guides
- Tertiary: Events, partnerships, collaborations
- Tone: Premium, artisanal, educational ("Craft baking since [year]")

**Engagement Analysis:**
- Average post likes: 100–250
- Engagement rate: ~2–3% (lower than Prime Burger)
- Comment sentiment: Mixed (compliments, but also "too expensive" feedback)
- User-generated content: Minimal

**Strengths:**
- Strong education angle (teaches followers about ingredients, process)
- Artisanal/craft positioning is clear
- Premium brand perception
- Blog/website complements social (owned media advantage)
- Ingredient sourcing story is compelling

**Weaknesses:**
- Lower posting frequency (less visible)
- Premium pricing alienates mass market
- Limited community interaction (educational but not conversational)
- No fun/casual moments (very serious brand)
- No location/accessibility messaging

**Opportunity for Lucky 13:**
- **Angle 1:** Accessible vs. premium
  - Bartels: Artisanal, expensive, for connoisseurs
  - Lucky 13: Quality ingredients, affordable, for everyone
  
- **Angle 2:** Fun & engaging vs. serious & educational
  - Bartels: Educational but formal ("Learn about sourdough fermentation")
  - Lucky 13: Fun storytelling ("Our lettuce is from a family farm 30km away")
  
- **Angle 3:** High frequency vs. low frequency
  - Bartels: Posts 2–3x/week
  - Lucky 13: **We post 4–5x/week with variety** (easier to stay top-of-mind)

## THE VEGAN TABLE vs COMPETITORS

**Finding:** No direct competitors of note in Phuket

**What this means:**
- Vegan Table has first-mover advantage in premium vegan dining (market leadership)
- Risk: No one to benchmark against locally
- Opportunity: Position as *the* vegan authority in Phuket

**Relevant indirect competitors (to monitor):**
- General wellness restaurants (if they add vegan options)
- International vegan chains (if they enter Phuket)
- Online delivery platforms (where vegan options are available)

**Strategic positioning for Vegan Table:**
- **Angle 1:** Local authority on plant-based dining
  - "10+ years pioneering vegan dining in Phuket"
  - "Award-winning chefs, award-winning values"
  - Position as thought leader, not just restaurant
  
- **Angle 2:** Community hub vs. transactional restaurant
  - Competitors (if they appear) will be transactional ("Order online, delivered")
  - Vegan Table: "Come dine with your people. Community table. Real values."
  
- **Angle 3:** Wellness + gourmet (not health code)
  - Competitors might emphasize health (risky, claims issues)
  - Vegan Table: Emphasize deliciousness + values + wellness

**Content strategy implication:**
- Feature thought leadership (why plant-based matters)
- Amplify community stories (customers, partners, suppliers)
- Showcase awards & history (credibility)
- Build email list aggressively (less reliant on social reach)
- Encourage user-generated content (customers becoming brand ambassadors)

## KEY COMPETITIVE GAPS WE'LL FILL

### For Lucky 13 Sandwich:
1. **Storytelling** — Competitors show products; we tell stories
2. **Frequency** — We post 4–5x/week; competitors post 2–3x/week (stay top-of-mind)
3. **Community** — We engage, respond, amplify customers; competitors broadcast
4. **Humor & personality** — We're fun; competitors are serious
5. **Local pride** — We emphasize Phuket roots; competitors are generic

### For Vegan Table:
1. **Thought leadership** — No competitors, so we become the authority
2. **Community building** — Focus on customer stories, wellness community
3. **Engagement cadence** — Consistent presence (2x/week minimum)
4. **Email capture** — Build list aggressively (less dependent on social algorithm)
5. **Awards & credibility** — Amplify 10-year history, past awards

### For Really Good Deli:
1. **B2B LinkedIn presence** — Competitors have ZERO LinkedIn; we dominate
2. **Thought leadership** — Position as expert on hotel/restaurant supplying
3. **Engagement** — Competitors post 1–2x/week; we post 3–5x/week
4. **Storytelling** — Competitors show products; we show relationships
5. **Dual B2B + B2C** — Competitors are retail only; we own both angles

## CONTENT STRATEGY IMPLICATIONS

### Lucky 13:
- **Post frequency:** 4–5x/week (vs competitors' 2–3x/week)
- **Content mix:** 40% product, 30% storytelling, 20% engagement, 10% user-generated
- **Tone:** Fun, casual, local-proud (vs competitors' premium/serious)
- **Differentiation:** Emphasize 5 locations, community, affordability

### Vegan Table:
- **Post frequency:** 3–4x/week (new benchmark; competitors don't exist)
- **Content mix:** 30% product, 30% wellness/community, 20% storytelling, 20% engagement
- **Tone:** Warm, educational, community-driven (vs generic)
- **Differentiation:** 10-year authority, awards, values-driven, community hub

### Really Good Deli:
- **Post frequency:** 3–4x/week (vs competitors' 1–2x/week)
- **Content mix:** 30% product (B2C), 30% thought leadership (B2B), 20% customer stories, 20% behind-the-scenes
- **Platforms:** LinkedIn primary (B2B), Instagram/Facebook secondary (B2C)
- **Tone:** Professional + warm (vs competitors' cold/transactional)
- **Differentiation:** 10-year hotel supplier expertise, local-first, dual B2B/B2C
