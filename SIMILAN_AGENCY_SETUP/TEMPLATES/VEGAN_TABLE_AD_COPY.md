## AD CREATIVE GUIDELINES

### Brand Colours (for designers)
- Primary green: Use brand hex code from brand guidelines
- Cream/off-white: Background for quote cards
- Dark text: High contrast for accessibility

### Typography
- Headline font: [Brand font — confirm with client]
- Body font: Clean sans-serif (Helvetica Neue, Inter, or brand equivalent)
- Always use white text on dark/image backgrounds with sufficient contrast

### Photography Style
- Warm, natural lighting preferred
- Dark surfaces (slate, wood) for dish photography
- Avoid stock photography — always use original The Vegan Table images
- Aspect ratios to always produce: 1:1 (feed), 4:5 (feed), 9:16 (Stories/Reels)

### What to Avoid
- Generic stock images of salads or smoothies
- Overly bright, saturated colours — this brand is warm and refined
- Health claim language: "detox," "cleanse," "superfood," "healing," "medicinal"
- Exaggerated urgency language: "HURRY!", "DON'T MISS OUT!!!"
- Before/after health imagery

## TESTING RECOMMENDATIONS

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4):** Run Ad #1, #4, #10, #13 simultaneously. Low budget, test creative performance.

**Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8):** Scale winning creatives. Introduce event ads (#7, #8) when event is confirmed.

**Phase 3 (Weeks 9–12):** Full campaign activation. Anniversary ads (create custom versions of #1 and #10 with 10-year messaging). Retargeting expanded to 60-day window.

**Key metrics to optimise for:**
- Awareness campaigns: CPM, Reach, 3-second video views
- Conversion campaigns: CPC, Link Clicks, Booking page visits
- Retargeting: CTR, Return visitor conversions

**Refresh creatives every 4–6 weeks** to avoid audience fatigue. Always have 2–3 backup creatives ready.
