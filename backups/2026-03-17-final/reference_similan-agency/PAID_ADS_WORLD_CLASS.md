## CORE COMPETENCIES (What Every Master Performance Marketer Knows)

### 1. FUNNEL ARCHITECTURE & STRATEGY

Understanding the customer journey:

**Awareness → Interest → Consideration → Conversion → Loyalty**

**Awareness Stage:**
- Goal: Get in front of right audience
- Metric: Reach, impressions, CPM (cost per 1000 impressions)
- Message: "Here's a brand solving [problem]"
- Platforms: Meta (broad targeting), Google (search intent)
- Example: Vegan Table awareness to wellness audience

**Interest Stage:**
- Goal: Make them curious, want to learn more
- Metric: Click-through rate (CTR), engagement rate
- Message: "Here's why we're different"
- Platforms: Meta (engagement ads), YouTube (video ads)
- Example: Lucky 13 showing behind-the-scenes, fresh ingredients

**Consideration Stage:**
- Goal: Convince them we're the best choice
- Metric: Conversion rate, cost per conversion
- Message: "Why we beat competitors + social proof"
- Platforms: Meta (conversion ads), Google (shopping)
- Example: Really Good Deli showing hotel clients, reviews, portfolio

**Conversion Stage:**
- Goal: Drive action (visit, order, buy)
- Metric: Conversions, ROAS (return on ad spend), CPA (cost per acquisition)
- Message: "Take action now [limited offer, free trial, etc.]"
- Platforms: Meta/Google (conversion-focused), Retargeting (warm audiences)
- Example: Lucky 13 "Order on Grab now" with link + map

**Loyalty Stage:**
- Goal: Keep customers coming back
- Metric: Repeat purchase rate, lifetime value
- Message: "Come back + refer friends"
- Platforms: Email, SMS, retargeting pixels
- Example: Vegan Table exclusive member offers, referral bonuses

**Strategy for 3 Brands:**

| Brand | Primary KPI | Funnel Focus | Budget Split |
|-------|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Lucky 13** | Foot traffic | Awareness + Conversion | 30% awareness, 70% conversion |
| **Vegan Table** | Brand awareness | Awareness + Consideration | 50% awareness, 50% consideration |
| **RGD** | B2B leads | Consideration + Conversion | 40% B2B lead gen, 60% B2C conversion |

### 3. CAMPAIGN STRATEGY & TESTING

Planning ads systematically:

**Campaign Hypothesis:**
Before launching, state: "We believe [audience segment] with [characteristic] will respond best to [message] because [reason]."

Example: "We believe professionals 25–35 who follow food blogs will respond best to 'fresh local sandwiches' messaging because they value quality + convenience."

**A/B Testing Framework:**
- **Test ONE variable at a time** (not multiple)
- **Sample size:** Run for 5–7 days, reach 1000+ people per ad
- **Measurement:** Compare CTR, conversion rate, ROAS
- **Winner:** 15%+ better performance = worth scaling

**Variables to test:**
1. **Audience:** Different interests, behaviors, demographics
2. **Creative:** Different images, videos, layouts
3. **Copy:** Different headlines, CTAs, messaging angles
4. **Offer:** Free trial vs discount vs "order now"
5. **Placement:** Desktop vs mobile vs Instagram stories vs Reels
6. **Budget:** $5/day vs $20/day (small scale vs scale)

**Testing sequence (first 30 days):**
- Week 1: Test 3 audiences (which resonates?)
- Week 2: Test 3 creatives (what visual wins?)
- Week 3: Test 3 copy angles (what message converts?)
- Week 4: Scale winner (push budget to best performer)

### 4. PLATFORM MASTERY: META (Facebook + Instagram)

Where most budget goes:

**Meta Ad Anatomy:**
- **Audience:** Who sees this ad
- **Placement:** Where it appears (feed, stories, reels, in-stream)
- **Creative:** Image or video
- **Copy:** Headline (25 char), primary text (125 char), description (30 char)
- **CTA Button:** "Learn More," "Shop Now," "Book Now," "Send Message"
- **Landing page:** Where click goes (website, Messenger, shop, etc.)

**Campaign Objectives:**
- **Awareness:** Reach (impressions) prioritized over conversions
- **Traffic:** Drive clicks to website
- **Engagement:** Likes, comments, shares on the ad itself
- **Conversions:** Optimize for purchase, form fill, registration
- **App installs:** If running app

**Best practices:**
- **Mobile-first:** 80% of Meta audience on mobile
- **Video beats image:** Video 3x higher engagement
- **Vertical video:** Reels 10x higher reach than feed
- **Short copy:** 1–2 sentences (longer = lower engagement)
- **Emojis:** Break up text visually, make it friendly
- **No-text images:** 20% text limit (Facebook rule, enforced)
- **Conversions pixel:** Track every action (clicks, adds to cart, purchases)
- **Retargeting:** Pixel everyone who visits website, re-target with different message

**Meta Budget Allocation (Example for Lucky 13):**
- 30% Awareness (broad interest targeting, high reach)
- 40% Conversion (warm traffic + retargeting, conversion-optimized)
- 20% Testing (new audiences, creatives, placements)
- 10% Loyalty (existing customer engagement)

### 5. PLATFORM MASTERY: GOOGLE ADS

For intent-driven conversion:

**Google Ads Types:**
- **Search:** Ads on Google search results (highest intent, highest CPC)
- **Shopping:** Product ads (eCommerce, high intent)
- **Display:** Banner ads across web (awareness, retargeting)
- **YouTube:** In-stream ads on videos
- **Performance Max:** AI optimizes across all Google channels

**Search Ad Anatomy:**
- **Keyword:** User searches "fresh sandwich Bangkok" → your ad shows
- **Headline:** 30 characters, mention keyword
- **Description:** 2 lines, 90 characters each, benefit + CTA
- **Landing page:** Highly relevant to search (don't send sandwich ad to homepage)
- **Bid:** How much you pay per click (smart bidding = Google sets bid)

**Keywords Strategy:**
- **Branded:** "Lucky 13 sandwich" (intent: high, your competitors bid on these)
- **Category:** "Fresh sandwich Phuket" (intent: high, less competition)
- **Broad:** "Best lunch in Phuket" (intent: medium, competitive)
- **Negative keywords:** "Frozen sandwich" (don't show ad for these)

**Quality Score (Google's rating):**
- 1–10 score (10 = perfect)
- Based on: Click-through rate, landing page relevance, ad relevance
- Lower score = higher CPC (costs more)
- Improve by: Better ad copy, better landing pages, better keyword match

**Google Budget Allocation (Example for RGD B2B):**
- 50% Branded keywords (customers looking for you by name)
- 30% Category keywords (buying intent, natural searches)
- 20% Testing (new keywords, placements)

### 6. CONVERSION OPTIMIZATION & LANDING PAGES

Ad is 50% of the equation; landing page is the other 50%:

**Landing Page Principles:**
- **Single goal:** One clear action (not multiple options)
- **Relevance:** Message on ad = message on landing page (no confusion)
- **Speed:** Page loads in <3 seconds (slow pages = bounces)
- **Mobile optimized:** Most traffic is mobile
- **Trust signals:** Reviews, testimonials, logos (credibility)
- **CTA prominent:** Button above the fold, clear action
- **Form fields:** Minimum fields (every field = 5% drop-off)

**Conversion Funnels:**
- **Single-page funnel:** One page, all info visible (fast, high abandon)
- **Multi-step funnel:** Step 1 (interest) → Step 2 (details) → Step 3 (purchase) (high conversion)
- **Quiz funnel:** Qualify audience + build engagement before offer (highest quality leads)

**Metrics to Track:**
- Landing page CTR (clicks to landing page / impressions)
- Landing page bounce rate (visitors who leave without action)
- Form completion rate (complete form / visitors)
- Cost per acquisition (ad spend / conversions)

### 7. BUDGET MANAGEMENT & OPTIMIZATION

Making every dollar count:

**Budget Structure:**
- **Total monthly budget:** Allocated across brands
- **Daily budget:** Total ÷ days (automates pacing)
- **Campaign budget:** Money allocated to specific campaigns
- **Bid strategy:** How you pay (manual vs automated)

**Daily Optimization Checklist:**
- Check spend (is it pacing correctly?)
- Check ROAS (is it meeting benchmark?)
- Pause underperforming ads (<1.0 ROAS)
- Increase budget on winners (>1.5 ROAS)
- Watch for issues (pixel errors, policy violations, account issues)

**Weekly Optimization:**
- Analyze performance data
- Identify winning audiences, creatives, copy angles
- Scale winners (increase budget 20–30%)
- Pause losers (below 1.2:1 ROAS)
- Plan next week's tests

**Monthly Review:**
- Total ROAS vs goal
- Cost per acquisition trend
- Customer lifetime value (are they worth it?)
- Test learnings (apply winners to next month)
- Adjust strategy for next quarter

### 8. DATA ANALYSIS & DECISION-MAKING

Numbers tell the story:

**Key Metrics by Campaign Type:**

**Awareness Campaigns:**
- **CPM:** Cost per 1,000 impressions (lower = better)
- **CTR:** Click-through rate (aim >1%)
- **Reach:** Total people reached
- **Frequency:** Avg times person sees ad (3–5 is sweet spot)

**Conversion Campaigns:**
- **CPA:** Cost per acquisition (total spend / conversions)
- **ROAS:** Return on ad spend (revenue / spend)
- **CVR:** Conversion rate (conversions / clicks, aim >2%)
- **AOV:** Average order value (revenue / orders)

**Benchmarks (Industry standards):**
- Facebook CTR: 0.5–1.5%
- Google ROAS: 1.5–3.0:1 (varies by industry)
- Lucky 13 target: $10–15 cost per foot traffic visit
- RGD target: $20–30 cost per qualified lead
- Vegan Table target: $0.50–1.00 cost per website visit

**Analysis Tools:**
- Meta Ads Manager (native)
- Google Ads dashboard (native)
- Excel/Sheets (tracking weekly performance)
- Attribution modeling (which touchpoint drove conversion?)

## QUALITY RUBRIC (Self-Scoring Before Launch)

Every campaign must pass this rubric before going live. Score 1–5 on each:

### Campaign Strategy (20 points total)

**Objective Clarity (5 points):**
- Clear KPI (foot traffic, leads, awareness, conversions)
- Benchmark set (what's "good" for this campaign?)
- Success metric defined (how will we measure success?)
- Budget allocated appropriately (not overspending on awareness if goal is conversion)

**Audience Definition (5 points):**
- Audience profile is detailed (not "everyone 18–65")
- Audience size reasonable (too small = can't scale, too big = not targeted)
- Interest/behavior targeting is precise (not overly broad)
- Audience data available (can we pixel them, retarget them?)

**Creative Strategy (5 points):**
- Hook is clear (first 3 seconds compelling, captures attention)
- Message supports objective (awareness ads look different than conversion ads)
- Offer is compelling (why should they click?)
- CTA is obvious (what action do we want?)

**Budget & Timeline (5 points):**
- Budget is allocated appropriately by stage (awareness vs conversion)
- Timeline is realistic (5–7 days minimum for testing)
- Benchmarks are based on data (not guesses)
- Contingencies planned (what if ROAS is 0.5:1?)

### Creative Execution (15 points total)

**Image/Video Quality (5 points):**
- High-quality creative (not blurry, not low-res)
- On-brand (colors, fonts match brand)
- Mobile-optimized (readable on phone)
- Attention-grabbing (not boring or generic)

**Copy Quality (5 points):**
- Headline is compelling (hooks in first line)
- Description is benefit-focused (not feature-focused)
- CTA is clear (specific action)
- No policy violations (no misleading claims, no restricted content)

**Platform Optimization (5 points):**
- Creative dimensions correct (1080×1080 for Instagram, not 500×500)
- Text coverage <20% (Facebook rules)
- Video length optimized (6–30 seconds for most platforms)
- Placement considered (mobile vs desktop, feed vs stories)

### Data & Measurement (15 points total)

**Tracking Setup (5 points):**
- Conversion pixel implemented (can we track conversions?)
- UTM parameters set (can we tie back to campaign?)
- Audience segments created (can we pixel and retarget?)
- Custom events set up (if applicable)

**Testing Plan (5 points):**
- A/B test variable identified (testing ONE thing)
- Sample size sufficient (1000+ people per ad)
- Test duration set (5–7 days minimum)
- Success criteria clear (how much better = worth scaling?)

**Performance Goals (5 points):**
- ROAS target realistic (based on benchmarks)
- Cost per action aligned with business model (sustainable?)
- Volume targets reasonable (not over-promising)
- Risk assessment done (what could go wrong?)
